‚ö†Ô∏è Rip Current Safety - CRITICAL!

Rip currents are the #1 hazard at beaches

If caught in a rip current:

  1. DON'T panic - stay calm and conserve energy
  2. DON'T swim against the current - you'll tire quickly
  3. Swim PARALLEL to shore until out of the current
  4. Once free, swim diagonally toward shore
  5. If you can't escape, float or tread water and wave for help

How to spot a rip current: Look for churning, choppy water, a line of foam/seaweed moving seaward, or a break in the wave pattern.

ü¶∫ Life Jacket Guidelines

  • Use U.S. Coast Guard-approved life jackets for children
  • Ensure proper fit - snug but comfortable, can't slip off
  • Children should wear life jackets in and around open water
  • Inflatable toys and floaties are NOT safety devices
  • Even with a life jacket, supervise children constantly
  • Check life jacket condition regularly (no rips, tears, or damage)

üåä Lake-Specific Safety

  • Be aware of underwater hazards (rocks, logs, debris)
  • Watch for sudden drop-offs - lake floors can be uneven
  • Avoid swimming near docks, boats, and boat traffic
  • Be cautious of aquatic plants and algae
  • Never dive into unfamiliar water - check depth first
  • Lightning safety: Get out at first sign of storm

üìû Emergency Response

If someone is in trouble:

  1. Alert a lifeguard immediately if available
  2. Call 911
  3. Reach or throw - don't go! Use a rescue device if possible
  4. Never enter the water unless you're a trained rescuer
