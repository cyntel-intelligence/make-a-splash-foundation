Critical statistics and prevention strategies

#1 Cause of Death

Drowning is the leading cause of death for children ages 1-4 in the United States

2nd Leading Cause

Second leading cause of unintentional injury death for children ages 5-14

Less Than 2 Minutes

A child can drown in the time it takes to answer the phone

Silent Killer

Drowning is often silent - no splashing, no waving, no calling for help

88% Reduction

Formal swim lessons reduce drowning risk by 88% for children 1-4 years old

1-2 Inches of Water

Young children can drown in as little as 1-2 inches of water

Age

  • Children 1-4 years: Highest drowning rates
  • Toddlers: Most vulnerable due to curiosity and lack of awareness
  • Teen boys: Higher risk due to overconfidence and risk-taking

Location

  • 75% of drownings for children under 5 occur in home pools
  • Bathtubs, buckets, toilets: Common drowning locations for infants/toddlers
  • Open water: Lakes, rivers, and oceans pose significant risks

Lack of Barriers

  • Most drownings occur when a child unexpectedly enters the water
  • Homes without 4-sided pool fencing have higher drowning rates
  • Unsecured pool gates and no alarms increase risk

Supervision Gaps

  • Most drownings happen when supervision lapses for less than 5 minutes
  • Distractions (phones, socializing) contribute to drownings
  • Adult may be present but not actively watching

LAYERS OF PROTECTION

No single strategy is enough. Use all layers together:

1. SUPERVISION (Most Important!)

  • Constant, focused supervision within arm's reach
  • Designate a "Water Watcher" - no distractions
  • Never leave children unattended near water
  • Know CPR - can save a life

2. BARRIERS

  • 4-sided pool fence (at least 4 feet high)
  • Self-closing, self-latching gates
  • Door and window alarms
  • Pool covers (but not substitute for fence)

3. SWIM LESSONS

  • Start as early as 6 months with survival swim training
  • Focus on survival skills: floating, breath control, self-rescue
  • Continue lessons through childhood
  • Practice year-round, not just summer

4. EMERGENCY PREPAREDNESS

  • Learn CPR and keep skills current
  • Keep rescue equipment poolside
  • Have a phone nearby for emergencies
  • Practice emergency drills

Prevention Works!

With proper precautions, most drownings are preventable
