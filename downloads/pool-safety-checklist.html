Pool Safety Checklist

Essential guidelines to keep children safe around pools

üèä Supervision (Most Important!)

  • Designate a "Water Watcher" who stays focused on the pool - no phone, no distractions
  • Keep children within arm's reach at all times - especially young children and non-swimmers
  • Never leave children unattended near water, even for "just a second"
  • Learn CPR - it can save a life in an emergency
  • Keep a phone poolside in case of emergency

üöß Barriers & Fencing

  • Install a 4-sided fence (at least 4 feet high) completely surrounding the pool
  • Use self-closing, self-latching gates that open outward
  • Keep the latch out of reach of young children (at least 54 inches high)
  • Ensure fence bars are no more than 4 inches apart
  • Remove furniture or objects that could be used to climb over the fence

üõü Safety Equipment

  • Keep rescue equipment poolside (shepherd's hook, ring buoy)
  • Have U.S. Coast Guard-approved life jackets for non-swimmers
  • Post CPR instructions in a visible location
  • Keep a first aid kit nearby
  • Install pool alarms on doors and gates leading to pool

üè° Pool Area Safety

  • Remove all toys from pool when not in use (toys attract children)
  • Secure pool cover completely when pool is not in use
  • Drain inflatable pools after each use
  • Empty all buckets and containers that collect water
  • Keep chemicals locked away and out of reach
  • Ensure deck surfaces are slip-resistant

üìö Education & Rules

  • Teach children to never swim alone
  • Establish and enforce pool rules (no running, no diving in shallow areas)
  • Enroll children in age-appropriate swim lessons
  • Teach children to ask permission before going near water
  • Practice emergency drills with your family

If a child is missing, check the pool FIRST!

Seconds count in drowning situations.

  1. Get the child out of the water immediately
  2. Call 911
  3. Begin CPR if trained and necessary
  4. Continue until emergency responders arrive
